Alpha Radiation


Alpha radiation is an ionising form of radiation, carrying between 4 and 9 mega electron volts (MeV) with each particle. An alpha particle is composed of 2 neutrons and 2 protons, resembling a helium nucleus that has been stripped of its electrons. Alpha particles are created by being emitted from the nucleus of a heavy radioactive element, such as uranium, radium and polonium, through a process called alpha decay. They are very energetic and have a positive charge (+2).


Alpha particles are highly ionising, but this also contributes to their weak penetration ability. They cannot even pass through a sheet of paper or human skin, so it is not dangerous to humans, unless ingested. If alpha radiation enters the human body, however, it causes usually significant internal damage and often death, even from very small doses.


In comparison, other forms of radiation such as gamma and beta radiation have greater penetration power, however they are not quite as dangerous if ingested. Both gamma and beta radiation can pass through the skin into the human body. Thick layers of lead, concrete or tungsten are needed to stop gamma rays.


Alpha radiation is infamously difficult to detect once inside the body. Alpha particles, unlike gamma rays, can not escape the body, meaning that conventional radiation monitoring techniques cannot detect internal alpha contamination. This contributed to the delayed diagnosis in the case of Alexander Litvinenko, who was poisoned using polonium-210, an alpha radiation emitter.

Quiz

Why can an alpha particle be called a helium ion?


When an atom loses or gains an electron, it becomes an ion. Alpha particles are identical to a helium nucleus (a helium atom without its electrons).

Why is alpha radiation the least and most dangerous type of radiation?


Alpha radiation is the most dangerous because it carries high energy, but it is the least dangerous externally because it has difficulty reaching areas where that energy can cause harm.

What type of shielding is required to protect against gamma radiation?


Thick layers of lead, concrete, or tungsten
